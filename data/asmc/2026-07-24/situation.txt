Scattered showers were observed over many parts of the ASEAN region today. A few to isolated hotspots were detected in the central parts of Viet Nam, the southern parts of Sumatra and Kalimantan, as well as in parts of Sumatra and Sulawesi. Smoke plumes were observed in the southwestern parts of Kalimantan and the eastern parts of Java. The hotspot and haze situation could not be determined due to extensive cloud cover.

Weather and Haze Outlook: Over the next few days, showers are forecast to prevail over most areas of the ASEAN region except for drier conditions forecast over the southern parts of Sumatra, many parts of Borneo as well as Java and the Lesser Sunda Islands. Isolated hotspots and smoke plumes can emerge in these area facing drier conditions. The prevailing winds over the Philippines are expected to remain strong due to Tropical Storm Noul. Otherwise, the prevailing winds over the Mekong sub-region are forecast to blow from the west. The prevailing winds over the southern ASEAN region are forecast to blow mainly from the southeast or south.
