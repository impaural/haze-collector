Dry conditions prevailed over most parts of the southern ASEAN region today, except over some parts of Peninsular Malaysia, the northern parts of Sumatra and the eastern parts of Borneo where isolated showers were observed. Meanwhile, rainfall was observed over the northern ASEAN region. Scattered hotspots were detected mainly in Kalimantan, while few to isolated hotspots were detected in Sumatra, Viet Nam, Java and the Lesser Sunda Islands. Moderate transboundary smoke plumes were observed to emanate from hotspots detected in West Kalimantan drifting northeastwards into western Sarawak. Slight to moderate localised smoke plumes were observed to emanate from hotspots detected in West and Central Kalimantan, the northern parts of Sumatra, as well as the eastern parts of Java. The full extent of the hotspot and smoke haze situation in the region could not be determined due to cloud cover.

Weather and Haze Outlook: In the coming days, dry conditions are expected over most parts of the southern ASEAN region, except over the northern and central parts of Sumatra, the northern and eastern parts of Borneo and Peninsular Malaysia where showers are forecast. On the other hand, wet weather is still expected to persist over the northern ASEAN region. The hotspots and smoke haze situation may escalate in areas experiencing pro-longed periods of drier weather. The prevailing winds over the southern ASEAN region are forecast to blow from the southeast to south. The prevailing winds over the northern ASEAN region are expected be blow from the southwest to west.
